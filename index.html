Experience

SynchroGrid

At SynchroGrid, I have worked on various projects involving the design and analysis of protective relay power systems for diverse clients. These projects included determining and validating relay settings for power system plans using Siemens CAPE, ensuring compliance with NERC standards, and leveraging SEL relay settings to calculate the necessary technical parameters. My work spanned transformer protection, line protection, and other critical areas. I also simulated power system designs to verify the performance of protection devices and conducted data analysis on ERCOT power system models. This analysis involved assessing updates to the power system plan—such as added buses and loads—and recommending adjustments to relay settings to maintain optimal protection and system reliability.

SAMSUNG

As the team lead for the Samsung Generator Testing Automation Project, my team and I successfully designed and implemented a digital control system that automates monthly generator tests, significantly enhancing operational efficiency and data management. This comprehensive system was developed for Samsung Austin Semiconductor and adhered to NFPA 110 standards.


Our solution integrated hardware, backend, and frontend subsystems. Key contributions included:


Android Application Development: Designed a robust application to trigger and monitor tests in real time, providing dynamic data visualization and enabling secure CSV exports for further analysis.


Backend Subsystem: Utilized Google Firebase for encrypted data storage and seamless communication between components.


Hardware Simulation Environment: Created an environment incorporating sensors to replicate real-world generator conditions for accurate testing.


These efforts culminated in achieving all project deliverables and winning First Place at the Board of Directors Competition.

SIEMENS

During my Summer 2023 internship at Siemens’ UL Research and Testing Lab in Atlanta, I contributed to multiple key projects focused on enhancing testing methodologies and advancing the lab's capabilities:


Dust Test Chamber Recommissioning: Spearheaded the redesign and recommissioning of the dust test chamber used for UL certification of control appliances. This involved resolving key issues with air pressure regulation, actuator lag, and circuit wiring. I implemented design improvements, including installing a new air regulator system, upgrading the actuator, and reassembling the dump gate. I conducted final tests to UL standards, ensuring the chamber's functionality while achieving significant cost savings compared to a full replacement.


DC Testing Capabilities: Contributed to expanding DC testing capabilities in anticipation of future clean energy demands. Designed and executed testing sequences for 400V and 600V DC equipment, focusing on calibration, endurance, and short circuit simulations. I evaluated internal vs. external testing solutions and proposed the installation of a load bank at the Tucker Lab to reduce dependency on external facilities.


Short Circuit Simulation Model: Collaborated with Siemens’ German team to support their short circuit simulation project. My role involved gathering critical data and understanding client requirements to assist in creating a comprehensive simulation program in Simulink. This model incorporated magnetic simulation data, rotor curves, and arc voltage data, enabling Siemens to optimize breaker designs and save on physical testing costs.


Throughout my internship, I gained hands-on experience with advanced testing equipment, collaborated with a multidisciplinary team, and delivered cost-effective solutions that improved lab efficiency and testing precision.

Hardware Trojans

This project addressed the growing threat of Hardware Trojans (HTs) in System-on-Chip (SoC) designs. These malicious modifications compromise chip security by leaking data, altering functionality, and degrading performance. The project introduced an innovative detection methodology leveraging Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to enhance HT detection accuracy and scalability.
